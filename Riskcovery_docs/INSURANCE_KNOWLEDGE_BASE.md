## 1. Fire & Consequential Loss

**Hebrew:** ביטוח אש מורחב ואבדן תוצאתי
**File:** כלל- אש ותוצאתי - ביט 2019 (2).pdf
**Insurer:** Clal Insurance | **Pages:** 48 | **Edition:** BIT 2019

### Coverage Trigger
Named perils (occurrence-based), with optional all-risks completion extension (3.20)

### Structure
- **Part A** — Property Insurance (ביטוח הרכוש)
- **Part B** — Business Interruption / Consequential Loss (אבדן תוצאתי)

### What Is Covered
**Part A:** Physical loss/damage to insured property from named perils:
- Fire, lightning, smoke, explosion
- Earthquake (optional), natural perils (optional: wind, flood, storm)
- Burst pipes/liquid damage
- Aircraft impact, vehicle impact
- Spontaneous combustion
- Strikes, riots, malicious damage
- Ground subsidence
- Corrosion/erosion

**Part B:** Loss of gross profit and wages during reinstatement period following a Part A insured event

### Key Exclusions
- Consequential loss (under Part A only)
- Electrical installation damage (limited to specific component)
- Marine-insured property
- Government-ordered damage
- Nuclear risks; War/terrorism
- Earthquake (unless purchased)
- Pollution (unless from insured peril)
- Gross negligence
- Intangible property/data

## 2. Mechanical Breakdown

**Hebrew:** ביטוח שבר מכני
**File:** כלל-שבר מכני - ביט 2019.pdf
**Insurer:** Clal Insurance | **Pages:** 19 | **Edition:** BIT 2019

### Coverage Trigger
All-risks for machinery (sudden, unforeseen physical damage)

### What Is Covered
Physical loss/damage to insured machinery and equipment requiring repair or replacement, at the insured's premises. Designed to complement (not overlap) the Fire policy.

### Key Exclusions
- Perils covered under Fire policy (complementary design)
- Consequential loss
- Overload/experimental use
- Manufacturer/supplier warranty liability
- Pre-existing defects
- Consumables/spare parts (bearings, belts, etc.)
- Foundations (unless listed)
- Wear/corrosion/natural deterioration
- War/terrorism; Nuclear
- Continued operation after known damage
- Theft; Intangible property; Gross negligence

### Extensions Available (11)

| Code | Name (EN) | Name (HE) |
|---|---|---|
| 3.1 | Explosion/Liquid/Burst Pipe Gap | פיצוץ/נוזלים/פקיעת צנרת |
| 3.2 | Electrical Panel Damage | נזק ללוח חשמל |
| 3.3 | Additional Necessary Expenses | הוצאות נוספות הכרחיות |
| 3.4 | Foundation Insurance | ביטוח יסודות |
| 3.5 | Accidental Gas Leak | דליפת גז תאונתית |
| 3.6 | Additional Property Found in Repair | רכוש נוסף שנמצא בתיקון |
| 3.7 | Adjacent/Connected Property Damage | נזק לרכוש סמוך/מחובר |
| 3.8 | Equipment Adaptation Expenses | הוצאות התאמת ציוד |
| 3.9 | Parts No Longer Available | חלקים שאינם זמינים |
| 3.10 | Property Additions | תוספות לרכוש |
| 3.11 | Sum Insured Reinstatement | השבת סכום ביטוח |

### Who Needs This
Any business heavily reliant on machinery — manufacturers, production plants, food processing, printing, textiles, metalworking, CNC machines, generators, compressors, HVAC, industrial robots, transformers, switchgear.

### Key Advisory Questions
1. Complete list and replacement values of all machinery?
2. Age and condition of equipment?
3. Maintenance schedule and service contracts?
4. Existing fire policy? (this is complementary)
5. Any specialized/imported equipment with hard-to-source parts?
6. What deductible per machine category?

## 3. Third Party Liability

**Hebrew:** ביטוח אחריות כלפי צד שלישי
**File:** כלל-צד שלישי - ביט 2019.pdf
**Insurer:** Clal Insurance | **Pages:** 17 | **Edition:** BIT 2019

### Coverage Trigger
Occurrence-based (unforeseen event causing damage during policy period)

### What Is Covered
Legal liability to pay third parties compensation for:
- Bodily injury, death, illness, mental/physical impairment
- Damage to/loss of third party property including direct consequential loss
- Reasonable legal defense costs (even beyond the liability limit)

### Key Exclusions
- Employee injury (→ Employers Liability)
- Motor vehicle liability
- Product liability (→ Product Liability policy)
- Professional liability; D&O liability
- Pollution (unless sudden/accidental)
- Property in insured's custody
- Contractual liability
- Nuclear; Radiation/EMF; War/terrorism
- Punitive damages; Asbestos; Gross negligence; Pure financial loss

Every business interacting with the public, with visitors, performing services, or operating in shared spaces. Required by virtually all commercial leases and service contracts in Israel.

## 5. Product Liability

**Hebrew:** ביטוח אחריות המוצר
**File:** כלל-מוצר - ביט 2019.pdf
**Insurer:** Clal Insurance | **Pages:** 17 | **Edition:** BIT 2019

### Coverage Trigger
**Claims-made** (claim filed during policy period, with retroactive date)

### What Is Covered
Legal liability for bodily injury, death, illness, or third-party property damage caused by a defective product manufactured, exported, imported, marketed, distributed, packaged, or supplied by the insured, after the product left the insured's control. Legal defense costs beyond the liability limit.

### Key Exclusions
- Employee injury; Contractual liability
- Manufacture contrary to law/standards
- Knowingly defective product
- Product unsuitability for intended use
- Product recall/replacement costs
- Damage to product itself or insured's own property
- Nuclear; Radiation/EMF; War/terrorism
- Prior knowledge of potential claims; Pre-retroactive date events
- Punitive damages; Pollution (unless sudden)
- Asbestos; Aircraft components; BSE; Genetically engineered products
- Human body-derived products; Gross negligence; Pure financial loss

### Extensions Available (6)

| Code | Name (EN) | Name (HE) |
|---|---|---|
| 4.1 | Vendors Endorsement | הרחבת מפיצים |
| 4.2 | Run-off/Discovery Period | תקופת גילוי (12 months) |
| 4.3 | Cross Liability | אחריות צולבת |
| 4.4 | Criminal/Admin Defense | הגנה פלילית/מנהלית |
| 4.5 | Personal Injury | פגיעה אישית (NIS 200K) |
| 4.6 | Work Performed by Insured | עבודות שבוצעו (completed operations) |

### Who Needs This
Manufacturers, importers, exporters, distributors, packagers, marketers of tangible products. Critical for food, industrial equipment, consumer goods, pharmaceuticals, and any supply chain business.

### Key Advisory Questions
1. What products manufactured/imported/distributed?
2. Annual turnover from products?
3. Export activity?
4. Retroactive date needed?
5. Prior product claims or known defects?
6. Downstream distributors needing vendor endorsement?
7. Perform installation/repair services?
8. Per-claim and aggregate limits needed?

## 6. Cash/Money

**File:** כלל-כספים - ביט 2019.pdf
**Insurer:** Clal Insurance | **Pages:** 13 | **Edition:** BIT 2019

All-risks (sudden, unforeseen, accidental loss/damage)

### What Is Covered
Physical loss/damage to business cash/money:
- Cash, banknotes, checks, postal orders, bills of exchange, promissory notes, securities, stamps

- During transit outside premises within territorial limits
- Burglary (break-in) and robbery (violence/threat)

### Key Exclusions
- Unexplained shortage
- Money in unsupervised vehicles
- Consequential loss
- Employee fraud/embezzlement (except messenger)
- Wear/deterioration
- Nuclear; War/terrorism
- On-premises theft that is not break-in or robbery
- Electronic payment instruments
- Forged checks/documents; Gross negligence

| 3.4 | Damage to Safe | נזק לכספת |

## 7. Fidelity/Crime

**Hebrew:** ביטוח נאמנות
**File:** כלל-נאמנות - ביט 2019.pdf
**Insurer:** Clal Insurance | **Pages:** 13 | **Edition:** BIT 2019

### Coverage Trigger
**Discovery-based** (damage discovered during policy period, with retroactive date)

### What Is Covered
Direct monetary loss caused by acts of employee dishonesty:
- Fraud, embezzlement, concealment, theft
- Committed in course of employment
- With intent to cause monetary damage or obtain unauthorized benefit
- All acts by a single employee treated as one loss

### Key Exclusions
- Pre-inception discovered damage
- Post-discovery damage (by same employee after discovery)
- Pre-retroactive date damage
- Damage not discovered within 12 months of employee departure
- Post-policy damage
- Consequential/indirect loss
- Nuclear; War/terrorism

### Extensions Available (1)

| Code | Name (EN) | Name (HE) |
|---|---|---|
| 4 | Discovery Period Extension | תקופת גילוי (6 months post-cancellation) |

### Who Needs This
Any business with employees who handle money, assets, or financial transactions. Important for cash handling businesses, financial institutions, retailers, warehouses, companies with access to bank accounts/payment systems.

### Key Advisory Questions
1. Number of employees with access to cash/assets/financial systems?
2. Total value of assets at risk?
3. Internal controls (dual signatures, audits)?
4. Prior employee dishonesty?
5. Retroactive date needed?
6. Per-occurrence and aggregate limits?

## 8. Cargo in Transit

### Coverage Trigger
All-risks (sudden, unforeseen, accidental loss/damage during transit)

### What Is Covered
Physical loss/damage to movable goods during transit by any vehicle within territorial limits:
- From loading onto vehicle to unloading at destination
- Includes loading/unloading and parking within buildings during journey
- Broad scope: includes cash, securities, explosives, live animals

### Key Exclusions
- Hazardous materials (unless owned by insured and lawful)
- Inherent defect/wear
- Porcelain/glass breakage (unless part of machinery)
- Container leakage; Consequential loss
- Theft not constituting break-in or robbery
- Unroadworthy vehicle; Unlicensed/impaired driver
- Employee fraud; War/terrorism; Nuclear
- Marine insurance overlap; Data/electronic info
- Gross negligence
- Overnight/unattended parking with cargo NOT covered

| 3.3 | Brand Protection/Salvage | הגנת מותג/הצלה |

Any business that regularly transports goods — logistics companies, distributors, manufacturers with delivery, retailers receiving shipments, importers/exporters, fleet operations.

## 9. Terrorism

**Hebrew:** ביטוח נזקי טרור (מעל מס רכוש)
**File:** פניקס- טרור - פסגה 2019.pdf
**Insurer:** Phoenix Insurance | **Pages:** 6 | **Edition:** Pisga 2019

### Coverage Trigger
Named peril (terror acts), occurrence-based. **Top-up policy** covering excess above Israeli Property Tax and Compensation Fund payments.

### What Is Covered
- **Chapter 1 (Property):** Physical damage from terror acts — difference between total damage and Property Tax Fund compensation
- **Chapter 2 (BI, optional):** Consequential loss/lost profits from terror events causing covered property damage
- Break-in/theft immediately following terror event
- Confiscation/seizure directly resulting from terror

### Key Exclusions
- War/civil war (non-terror armed conflict)
- Biological/chemical weapons (unless medical/research)
- Break-in/theft NOT immediately following terror
- Property at military/security installations
- Nuclear; Events outside geographic limits
- Consequential loss (under Chapter 1)
- Items not covered under standard fire policy
- Marine-insured property

### Who Needs This
Any Israeli business with property value exceeding the Property Tax and Compensation Fund cap. Essential for high-value properties, businesses in elevated terror risk areas.

### Key Advisory Questions
1. Total insurable property value?
2. Expected Property Tax Fund compensation (baseline)?
3. Differential/gap needing coverage?
4. Business interruption coverage needed?
5. Acceptable indemnity period?
6. Location risk assessment?
7. Existing fire policy?

## 10. Electronic Equipment

**Hebrew:** ביטוח ציוד אלקטרוני
**File:** כלל-ציוד אלקטרוני - ביט 2019.pdf
**Insurer:** Clal Insurance | **Pages:** 34 | **Edition:** BIT 2019

### Coverage Trigger
All-risks (occurrence-based) — "Loss or physical damage, sudden, unforeseen and unexpected, from any cause not excluded"

### Structure (4 Chapters)
- **Chapter 1** — Property Insurance (core, always active)
- **Chapter 2** — Data & Software Restoration Costs (optional)
- **Chapter 3** — Additional Operating Expenses (optional)
- **Chapter 4** — Consequential Loss / Business Interruption (optional)

### What Is Covered
**Chapter 1:** Electronic equipment including external storage media — whether in operation, at rest, being dismantled for cleaning/overhaul, or during transit within territorial limits. Valuation: new-for-old replacement cost.

**Chapter 2:** Costs of restoring data and software lost due to a Chapter 1 event. Optional "Comprehensive Software Cover" adds: communication failures, power surges, defective software, malicious hacking, viruses, faulty operation.

**Chapter 3:** Additional operating expenses for substitute/alternative means during downtime (daily indemnity limit).

**Chapter 4:** Loss of profit (gross profit + salary costs) during business interruption from a Chapter 1 event.

### Key Exclusions
**Chapter 1:**
- Residual policy exclusion (if Fire policy covers the item)
- Pre-existing defects (unless unknown)
- Wear/tear/corrosion from normal use
- Repair of operational defects; Routine maintenance
- Manufacturer/supplier/installer warranty liability
- Property on aircraft or vessels
- Consequential loss (unless Chapter 4)
- Consumables/replaceable materials
- Aesthetic/cosmetic defects
- Simple theft (must be burglary or robbery)

**Chapter 2:** Beta/unreleased software; unlicensed software; incompatible media treatment; manual data entry errors; no 2-generation backup; no anti-virus/OS updates; no UPS

## 12. Contractor Works (CAR)

**Hebrew:** ביטוח עבודות קבלניות/הקמה
**File:** כלל-עבודות קבלניות - ביט 2019.pdf
**Insurer:** Clal Insurance | **Pages:** ~30 | **Edition:** BIT 2019

### Coverage Trigger
All-risks (occurrence-based) — "Loss or physical damage, accidental and unforeseen, from any cause not excluded"

### Structure (3 Chapters)
- **Chapter A** — Property Insurance (ביטוח הרכוש)
- **Chapter B** — Third Party Liability (אחריות כלפי צד שלישי)
- **Chapter C** — Employer's Liability (אחריות מעבידים)

### What Is Covered
**Chapter A:** Replacement, repair, or re-erection of insured works — construction/civil engineering works, erection works, materials, equipment, systems at the work site.

**Chapter B:** Third party liability for bodily injury, death, illness, property damage arising from unforeseen events during execution of insured works. Includes legal defense costs.

**Chapter C:** Employer's liability for employee bodily injury, harm, illness, death occurring during employment in the insured works. Plus criminal/administrative defense (NIS 200K/event, NIS 400K aggregate).

### Key Exclusions

**Chapter A:** Consequential/indirect loss; faulty design/materials/workmanship (but resultant damage to sound parts covered); wear/tear; mechanical breakdown (except running-in); cash/securities; theft when no workers present; unexplained shortage

**Chapter B:** Insured's own property; employee injury (→ Ch C); insured's property; motor vehicle liability; contractual liability; underground items; pollution (except sudden); vibration/support weakening; punitive damages; asbestos; pure financial loss

**Chapter C:** Contractual liability; National Insurance claims; asbestos; silicosis; motor vehicles/road accidents; punitive damages

**General (all chapters):** War/terrorism; nuclear; work stoppage > 90 days; overseas damage; turnkey/BOT defects; unlicensed crane operators; gross negligence

## Cross-Policy Relationships

### Complementary Pairs
- **Fire + Mechanical Breakdown**: Mechanical Breakdown explicitly excludes Fire perils; designed to work together
- **Fire + Terrorism**: Terrorism is a top-up above Property Tax Fund; references Fire policy
- **TPL + Employers Liability**: TPL excludes employee injury; EL covers it
- **TPL + Product Liability**: TPL excludes product liability; separate policy needed
- **Electronic Equipment + Fire**: Electronic Equipment can be "residual" (covering only what Fire doesn't)

### Bundled Coverage (CAR)
- **CAR** combines Property + TPL + EL in one policy for construction projects
- Separate standalone TPL and EL still needed for non-construction operations

### Coverage Gaps to Watch
1. **Professional Liability (E&O)** — Not covered by any of the 12 policies
2. **Directors & Officers (D&O)** — Not covered
3. **Cyber Insurance** — Not covered (Electronic Equipment covers physical damage only, not cyber liability)
4. **Environmental/Pollution Liability** — Only sudden/accidental pollution covered under TPL
5. **Marine Insurance** — Explicitly excluded from multiple policies
6. **Motor Vehicle** — Excluded from liability policies; requires separate motor policy

## Questionnaire Design Guide

### Tier 1: Universal Questions (All Clients)

1. **Business Type/Industry** → Determines mandatory policies (see Industry Matrix)
2. **Number of Employees** → Triggers Employers Liability (mandatory for all employers)
3. **Annual Revenue/Turnover** → Sizing for BI coverage (Fire Part B, Electronic Equipment Ch 4)
4. **Physical Premises** (own/rent, type, size) → Fire policy sizing
5. **Property Value** (buildings, contents, machinery, inventory) → Fire policy sums
6. **Location** (city, area, zone) → Territorial limits, earthquake/terror risk

### Tier 2: Activity-Based Questions

7. **Do you manufacture/distribute products?** → Product Liability
8. **Do you handle cash?** (max on-premises, max in-transit) → Cash/Money
9. **Do you own/use heavy machinery?** → Mechanical Breakdown
10. **Do you own/use electronic equipment?** (servers, data center, IT) → Electronic Equipment
11. **Do you own/operate heavy/engineering equipment?** → Heavy Equipment
12. **Are you involved in construction/renovation?** → Contractor Works (CAR)
13. **Do you transport goods?** → Cargo in Transit
14. **Do you import/export?** → Cargo in Transit (Extension 3.2)

### Tier 3: Risk-Specific Questions

15. **Employees handle money/assets?** → Fidelity/Crime
16. **Subcontractors used?** → TPL ext 4.4, EL ext 4.6
17. **Public visitors on premises?** → TPL sizing
18. **Send employees abroad?** → TPL/EL abroad extensions
19. **Employees carry weapons?** → TPL/EL weapons extensions
20. **Near water bodies?** → Heavy Equipment exclusion
21. **Underground utilities?** → CAR extension 7.2
22. **Earthquake zone?** → All property policies earthquake option

### Tier 4: Coverage Depth Questions (Per Policy)

Once a policy type is identified as needed, ask policy-specific questions to determine:
- Sum insured / liability limits
- Deductible preferences
- Which extensions to include
- Specific risk factors that affect pricing
